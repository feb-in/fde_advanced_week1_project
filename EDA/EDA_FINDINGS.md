#### `change`

missing 0.0%· none· lift 1.06×· conf high

- **Meaning.** Binary administrative/clinical flag indicating whether there was a CHANGE in the patient's diabetic medications during this encounter. 'Ch' = at least one antidiabetic drug was added/started/stopped or had its dose adjusted (Up/Down) among the 23 per-drug columns; 'No' = no change to the antidiabetic regimen. It is an encounter-level roll-up derived from the per-drug medication columns, capturing the fact that the care team adjusted the regimen during the stay.
- **Distribution.** Clean binary, fully populated, verified against indicator.json: 'No' = 54,755 rows (53.805%) and 'Ch' = 47,011 rows (46.195%); counts sum to 101,766. Near-balanced 54/46 split, so high-variance and well-populated (no rare-level / near-constant concern, is_constant=false, n_unique=2). Literal strings 'No'/'Ch' with no '?' placeholder. NOTE: these counts are on the FULL unfiltered 101,766-row table; the R5 expired/hospice drop (2,423 rows) is applied upstream and will shift counts/rates marginally.
- **vs. target.** Real but weak signal in the expected direction, and the prior analysis MIS-FRAMED its magnitude. Positive (30-day readmit) rate is 0.11823 for 'Ch' vs 0.10591 for 'No'. The facts-file target_lift of 1.059 is Ch's rate RELATIVE TO THE OVERALL BASE RATE (0.11823/0.1116=1.059), NOT the Ch-vs-No comparison. The correct Ch-vs-No relative difference is 0.11823/0.10591 = 1.116 (~11.6% relative, ~12% vs ~11% absolute); 'No' sits at 0.949x base rate. So a changed regimen is associated with modestly higher readmission, plausibly flagging a sicker/less-stable patient. Still a weak standalone separator; value is as one contributor and in interactions (change x num_medications x diabetesMed).
